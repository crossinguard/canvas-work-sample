Objective

To introduce the Course Evaluation Checklist and identify areas of focus to improve digital learning

Course Evaluation

In 2019, Instructure released an updated checklist of best practices when designing a digital course on Canvas. Creating a successful and accessible online learning environment, despite many shared goals with a physical environment, relies on skills more in the wheelhouse of a frontend web developer than that of a classroom teacher. This checklist helps to bridge the skill gap by combining pedagogical best practices with the accessibility demands of modern web design.

The Course Evaluation Checklist v2.0 contains best practices for elevating the quality of Canvas courses across four areas of focus, with each area discussed in its own module.

  1. Course Information
  2. Course Content
  3. Assessment of Student Learning
  4. Course Accessibility

Whether looking to create a fresh course or update existing materials, these areas of focus provide guidelines for presenting information to learners. This course will investigate each of these areas of focus, identify relevant checklist items, and provide functional examples demonstrating their use. Different institutions serve various populations with diverse needs. While business requirements and style guidelines may differ from this checklist, meaningful considerations when designing a modern online learning environment can still be gleaned from this course.

Please note that while this course represents my own interpretation, the core knowledge and underlying principles must be attributed to the checklist contributors Deonne Johnson, Erin Keefe, Lily Philips, and Michelle Lattke. You can find their original work and many related resources in the Resources module.
