Class Expectations, Policies, and Requirements

1.4 | Class expectations, policies, and technology requirements are provided, including participation rules, etiquette expecations, code of conduct, grading, late work, make-up work, and technology needs. UDL8.1UDL8.3

The intentional, transparent presentation of class expectations enables students to make informed decisions and uphold their responsibilities in the course. This helps to distribute responsibility and workload to all knowledge stakeholders and create an environment more conducive to learning.

What Does It Look Like?

Set clear expectations with upfront rules and policies. Outline both the negative consequences if expectations are not met and the positive benefits of meeting them. Doing so helps keep the workload of educators manageable regarding late work, absences, and make-up work while promoting accountability in students.
